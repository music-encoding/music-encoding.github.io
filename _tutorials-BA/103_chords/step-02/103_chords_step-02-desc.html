Well done! Now it's time to make a <chord> out of these three <note>s. As mentioned in the previous step, you should enclose the note elements in a chord element and move the duration attributes from the notes to the chord.

In the editor below, enclose the three <note> elements in a <chord> element (i.e. insert the opening tag of <chord> before the first note and the closing tag after the last one). The three single notes should now be rendered not sequentially, but vertically aligned, but they lack a correct duration yet. So remove the duration attributes from all the notes and add one to the <chord> instead. Keep the duration of a quarter (@dur="4").

You should then see the chord rendered. Again, you may play a little bit with the attribute values to see how they influence the rendering. You will also see hints on how to write the correct code.
